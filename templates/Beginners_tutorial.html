Welcome to Stock Marketing

Learn the basics of stock trading and investment strategies.

What is Stock Marketing?

  1. The stock market is a financial marketplace where shares of publicly traded companies are bought and sold.
  2. Stocks, also called equities, represent partial ownership in a business.
  3. Investors earn profits through:
    • Dividends – Profit-sharing by the company.
    • Capital appreciation – Increase in stock value over time.
    • Stock prices fluctuate, and if a company underperforms, investors may face losses.

What are Stocks?

Stocks are an investment method to build wealth. When you invest in the stock of a company, it means you own a share in the company that issued the stock. Stock investment is a way to invest in some of the most successful companies. There are different types of stocks available in the market categorized based on the following criteria:

  1. Market capitalization
  2. Ownership
  3. Fundamentals
  4. Price volatility
  5. Profit sharing
  6. Economic trends

How Stocks are Issued and Traded?

Public companies issue stocks to raise funds for expansion, new projects, and operations. Once issued, stocks are traded in the secondary market, where existing shareholders can sell their shares to new investors. Trading happens on regulated stock exchanges under the supervision of SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India).

Stock Exchanges in India

The two primary stock exchanges in India are:

  • Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) – Established in 1875, it is Asia’s oldest stock exchange.
  • National Stock Exchange (NSE) – Established in 1992, it is the largest by trading volume.
All trading takes place electronically through stockbrokers and online platforms.

How to Invest in the Indian Stock Market?

To start investing, an individual needs:

  • Demat Account – Stores shares in electronic form.
  • Trading Account – Facilitates buying and selling of stocks.
  • SEBI-Registered Stockbroker – An intermediary for executing trades.
Investors can trade stocks through online brokerage platforms, mobile apps, and stock market terminals.

Factors Influencing Stock Prices

Factors include:

  • Company performance – Profits, revenue, and management decisions.
  • Economic conditions – Inflation, GDP growth, and interest rates.
  • Government policies – Tax regulations, trade policies, and sector reforms.
  • Global market trends – International stock market movements and geopolitical events.

How to Analyze Stocks in the Stock Market?

Stock market analysis is crucial for making informed investment decisions. It helps investors choose the right stocks based on various financial, technical, and market factors. In India, stock analysis can be broadly categorized into:

  • Fundamental Analysis – Evaluating a company’s financial strength and growth potential.
  • Technical Analysis – Studying price trends, patterns, and trading volume.
  • Sentiment Analysis – Understanding market behavior and news impact.

Fundamental Analysis

Fundamental analysis helps investors assess whether a stock is undervalued or overvalued based on financial data, industry trends, and economic conditions. It is ideal for long-term investors.

Technical Analysis

Technical analysis is used to predict short-term price movements based on historical data, stock charts, and indicators. It is widely used by traders.

Key Tools & Indicators in Technical Analysis

  • Stock Price Charts
    • Line Chart – Shows stock price trends over time.
    • Candlestick Chart – Displays open, close, high, and low prices for a specific period.
  • Trend Analysis
    • Uptrend – Stock price is continuously rising.
    • Downtrend – Stock price is continuously falling.
    • Sideways Trend – Stock price fluctuates within a limited range.
  • Support & Resistance Levels
    • Support Level: A price level where a stock usually does not fall below.
    • Resistance Level: A price level where a stock struggles to rise above.
